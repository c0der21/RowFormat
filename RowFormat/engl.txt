Alexander Grigoryevich Lukashenko (Belarusian: Алякса́ндр Рыго́равіч Лукашэ́нка, romanized: Alyaksándr Ryhóravich Lukashénka, Russian: Алекса́ндр Григо́рьевич Лукаше́нко, born 30 August 1954) is a Belarusian politician serving as President of Belarus since the office was created on 20 July 1994.[2] Before launching his political career, Lukashenko worked as director of a collective farm (kolkhoz) and spent time with the Soviet Border Troops and the Soviet Army. He was the only deputy to vote against the independence of Belarus from the Soviet Union.
